AGENCY FUNCTIONS AND ROLES

Museums play an important role in the development of society and the country. The contribution and role expressed through the activities can be seen from several aspects.

1. Museums as National Historical and Cultural Treasures

The museum has a high commitment and sense of responsibility in saving and preserving human heritage and its environment. Rescue and conservation activities are followed up with complete information and documented before being published and published for distribution to the public. Accordingly, the museum acts as a searcher and gatherer of information for the public.

2. Museums as Disseminators of Information and Educating the Community

Disseminating information and educating the public about certain fields is one of the main functions of the Museum. Exhibitions, which are planned and held several times throughout the year, are a way to attract people to the Museum. The results of research and scientific studies carried out are published in the form of books, monographs, journals and others to be distributed and channeled knowledge to society as a whole. This method is expected to produce a society that loves science and is sensitive to its own history and culture. Dissemination of information by the Terengganu State Museum Board, taking into account all levels of society, because the Museum adheres to the community-friendly concept "If they cannot come to the Museum, the Museum has to go to them". Therefore, the State Museum also plans a program of traveling exhibitions to schools, giving talks, seminars and more.

3. Cultivating Community Awareness of Their Own History and Culture

The Museum's activities are in line with the goal of creating a spirit of awareness, and the community's love for its own cultural heritage. The conservation efforts and research carried out can open the eyes of the community, how necessary cultural heritage is to be preserved. At the same time, lectures, seminars and workshops were also held to teach and educate the community about the ways and importance of preserving cultural heritage.

4. Museums as Institutions of Education and Learning

The museum, which acts as an educational and learning institution, provides a number of specific programs such as:

  • Guided Tours: The Museum provides officers and experienced staff to provide information and explanations about the collections on display to visitors, especially school students.
  • Lectures: The Museum is responsible for giving lectures or explanations about its function and role as well as its importance to the community. This step is expected to give awareness and identity to the new generation of the greatness and tenacity of their ancestors once upon a time.
  • Workshops: The Museum also organizes school holiday programs by holding workshops to train school students such as exhibition preparation workshops, conservation workshops and others.
  • Promotional Publications: The Museum also acts as an information disseminating agent, through the distribution of brochures, bulletins and postcards containing brief information related to exhibitions, historical places, activity calendars and so on.
  • Cultivating the Spirit of Belonging and Nationalism: The museum, which is likened to an 'open university' through the exhibits on display, can give every member of the community the opportunity to get to know their own culture or that of another nation. This can foster a sense of belonging. Visitors also have the opportunity to take lessons from past history, about the importance of past figures and national heroes, and even the development of technology. This situation can indirectly foster nationalism, especially among the younger generation.

Library and Exhibitions

Along with its function and role as information dissemination, the museum also provides a library which contains various books, papers and publications that can be used as reference material. The Museum also holds various exhibitions, either permanent or mobile, which aim to disseminate information about history and culture. This method can create a knowledgeable and creative society as well as innovation.
