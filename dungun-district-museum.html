Dungun District Museum

Dungun District Museum Establishment History

The inspiration for the establishment of the District Museum arose from the launch of the East Coast Economic Region (ECER) by YAB Prime Minister of Malaysia on 28 October 2007 which emphasized the promotion of coastal tourism, islands, culture and heritage. The Terengganu State Museum Board proposed to the State Government that Dungun District be set up as a Museum because since the 1930s, it has been known throughout the world related to iron ore mining activities in Bukit Besi, Dungun. The 'soft opening' ceremony of the Dungun District Museum was launched on 19 August 2008.

Dungun District Officer's House History

The Dungun District Officer's House was built in 1941 and measures 24.5m long and 6.63m wide. The house, which now looks dilapidated, is made of wood and has a cement roof. It was once the temporary residence of the British Assistant Adviser. Dungun District officials who lived here were Tuan Haji Dol Abdullah bin Alias ​​(1941-1942), Che Khalid bin Kassim (1943-1945), Haji Khalid bin Awang (1974-1975) and many others.

Objectives of the Establishment of a District Museum

  • Preserving historic buildings for future generations to see
  • Cultivating the spirit of recognizing and appreciating the architecture of the past
  • Cultivating the spirit of appreciation of local historical heritage
  • As a reference and research source for School, College, University students and individuals
  • Cultivating a sense of pride in the creativity of previous generations as well as awakening the nostalgia of the past
  • Make it a District Museum whose main focus is on the history and culture of the district
  • Be an asset to the tourism industry
