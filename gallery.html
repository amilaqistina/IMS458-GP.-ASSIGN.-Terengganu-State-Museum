Gallery

Islam Gallery

The Islam Gallery showcases a rich collection of Islamic art and artifacts, highlighting the profound cultural and historical contributions of Islam to the region.

Witness the uniqueness of Terengganu Carved Stones, a discovery that is proof of the spread and glory of Islam and the beginning of Jawi writing in the archipelago.

Craft Gallery

The Craft Gallery celebrates the traditional crafts of Terengganu, featuring exquisite examples of local handiwork, including textiles, ceramics, and woodwork.

Learn how to make copper items in a very realistic place and get to know the various products.

Petronas Gallery

The Petronas Gallery displays exhibits related to the oil and gas industry, showcasing the technological advancements and historical development of this vital sector in Malaysia.

Visitors can see for themselves the shape of an oil rig built in the middle of the sea as well as the life of the workers on the rig.

History Gallery

The History Gallery offers a comprehensive look at the history of Terengganu, from its ancient roots to its modern-day achievements, through a variety of artifacts and exhibits.

The clash of two cultures through Chinese ceramics famous for its artwork.

Exemplifying and appreciating Bewah Cave Archaeological Site all the services and devotion that they have poured in bringing great changes to the state of Terengganu.

Royal Gallery

The Royal Gallery presents the opulent history and heritage of the Terengganu royalty, featuring royal regalia, portraits, and other significant artifacts.

The Royal Gallery which also exhibits the uniqueness of the twisted dagger belonging to the sultan.

Textile Gallery

The Textile Gallery displays a stunning collection of traditional and contemporary textiles, highlighting the rich textile heritage of Terengganu.

The Longest Handwritten Batik measuring 200 meters long and 1.08 meters wide has received world recognition and has been recorded in the 'World Guiness of Record' book and is also exhibited in this gallery.
